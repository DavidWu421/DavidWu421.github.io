Hello! I am a third year PhD student at the [Center for Gravitational Physics](https://weinberg.utexas.edu/research/center-gravitational-physics) at the University of Texas at Austin working under Professor Aaron Zimmerman. My interests are in General Relativity, black holes, and gravitational waves. More specifically, I focus on black hole perturbation theory and the ringdown portion of the gravitational wave signals from binary black hole mergers. My current projects are computing quasinormal modes in beyond-GR theories as well as understanding the near extremal structure of beyond-GR quasinormal modes.

Before coming to UT Austin, I earned my B.A. in physics from Cornell University. While there, I worked as part of the [SXS Collarboration](https://www.black-holes.org/) under Professor Saul Teukolsky in Numerical Relativity. However, my first area of research was in physics education in the [Cornell Physics Education Research Lab](https://www.lassp.cornell.edu/natasha-holmes-group) with Professor Natasha Holmes. If you've never read any Physcis Education Research (PER) papers before, I strongly recommend it! In particular, research on gender and racial inequalities within physics can be quite eye opening and shows we can always be working towards making the field more welcoming to all!

Outside of physics, you can catch me playing ultimate frisbee in my free time, having played for both Cornell's and UT's club ultimate teams. If you're ever looking for a new sport to try, the ultimate community is one of the most beginner friendly communities you'll find!
